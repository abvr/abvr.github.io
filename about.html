Abhinav Rajagopalan

I work on systems programming involving operating systems, compilers, languages where I try to build performant systems and tackle their challenging problems.

During the day, I mostly work with the Linux kernel and it's ecosystem and try to make some sense of all it's complexity.

Over the years I have been growing interested in computer science and some of it's subfields like FSM, automata, graph theory and other conceptually arcane topics.

While exploring the deep space, I've been trying not to get intimidated by the depth and vastness of the various topics in science, say for example I recently grew interested in cellular automata, which is at the brink of computing and biology with a bit of physics and mathematics at the foundation of it all.

In my precious spare time I'm mostly try to work on my hobby projects involving some archaic methods, and progress with independent research in the physics that I enjoy subsuming in, and whatever else strikes my fancy.

I did my bachelors in EE under Anna University.

Research

My interests in computing include old school UNIX systems, compilers, languages, AGI, Hidden Markov model, cryptography, Turing machines, combinatorics, λ-calculus and analysis of algorithms.

Much of my research is devoted to theoretical and relativistic physics, and currently revolves around manifolds, differential geometry, GR.

I also like to occasionally dabble in some observational astronomy and computational biology recreationally.
